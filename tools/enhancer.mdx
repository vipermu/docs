The Enhancer Tool lets you improve both images and videos — sharpening details, upscaling resolution, and refining style or motion. It works with any file from your Assets or uploads from your device.

## How to Enhance (Quick Flow for Beginners)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload or Select">
    Upload or select an image or video.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Pick Your Upscaler">
    Choose from:
    - **Krea Video (basic)** → faster, lighter, up to 4K / 40s
    - **Topaz Video (advanced)** → professional-grade, up to 8K / 60s, higher compute
    - **Image Enhancer** → up to 22K for still images
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose Enhancement Model">
    Select the appropriate model for your content (see table below).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Frame Interpolation (Optional)">
    Turn on Frame Interpolation for smoother motion (30–120 fps).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enhance">
    Click Enhance → download or send to another tool.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Video Upscaler Models

### Topaz Video

<Accordion title="Topaz Video (Professional-Grade)">
  - **Industry-grade AI upscaler** integrated into Krea
  - **Supports up to 8K resolution** and clips up to 60 seconds
  - **Adds frames, grain, and focus fixes** with high fidelity
  - **Labeled as an expensive model** because it consumes more compute
  - **Best for professional / production-quality footage**
</Accordion>

### Krea Video

<Accordion title="Krea Video (Native Generative)">
  - **Krea's native generative upscaler**
  - **Supports up to 4K resolution** and clips up to 40 seconds
  - **Focuses on adding new details** and improving visual quality
  - **Faster and lighter than Topaz**, better for everyday or creative work
</Accordion>

## Topaz Video (Advanced Upscaler)

Topaz Video is a professional-grade model with more advanced parameters for detail restoration and artifact correction.

### Key Features:
- **Supports up to 8K resolution** and clips up to 60s
- **Includes advanced controls** (model selection: Artemis, Gaia, Proteus, etc.)
- **Offers Focus Fix, Noise Reduction, and Frame Interpolation** options
- **Consumes more compute**, labeled as an expensive model
- **Best for professional work** or footage requiring maximum fidelity

### Video Enhancement Options for Topaz

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Basic Settings">
    - **Resolution** → Set target resolution (up to 8K)
    - **Enhancement toggle** → Enable/disable AI video enhancement

  <Accordion title="Advanced Options">

    - **Grain toggle** → Adds subtle film grain for realism
  </Accordion>

## Video Enhancement Models (Detailed)

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Artemis">
    **Designed for general video enhancement.**
    
    - Good for removing noise, sharpening, and restoring overall detail
    - Balanced between speed and quality
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Gaia">
    **Focuses on maximizing detail and adding texture.**
    
    - Best for upscaling low-resolution videos
    - May introduce stronger AI-generated detail (less faithful to the original)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Iris">
    **Specialized in enhancing faces and people.**
    
    - Works well for interviews, vlogs, or any footage where human features are the focus
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Nyx">
    **Optimized for low-light or dark footage.**
    
    - Reduces noise and preserves shadow detail
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Proteus">
    **The most flexible, with tunable parameters.**
    
    - Adjustable sharpness, noise reduction
    - Great for fine-tuning depending on your footage
    - Often considered the "pro" choice for users who want control
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Rhea">
    **Focused on stability and smoothness.**
    
    - Good for footage with lots of camera movement
    - Reduces jitter and motion artifacts
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Theia">
    **A hybrid model that adapts automatically to the content.**
    
    - Tries to balance detail, sharpness, and noise reduction without heavy manual tweaking
    - Good for users who want a "smart auto" option
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Frame Interpolation Models (Detailed)

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Apollo">
    **High-quality frame interpolation model.**
    
    - Produces smooth motion while preserving detail and minimizing artifacts
    - Best for general use when quality matters more than speed
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Apollo Fast">
    **A faster version of Apollo.**
    
    - Sacrifices some accuracy for quicker renders
    - Useful for previews or when speed is more important than perfection
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Chronos">
    **Specialized in creating new frames for slow motion effects.**
    
    - Works best when slowing down footage by 2×, 4×, or more
    - Preserves temporal consistency better than Apollo
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Chronos Fast">
    **Faster variant of Chronos with reduced detail accuracy.**
    
    - Good for quick drafts or less demanding footage
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Chronos Slo-Mo / FPS Conversion">
    **Advanced version of Chronos built for precise frame rate conversion.**
    
    - Converts between arbitrary FPS (e.g., 24 → 60 fps) while keeping motion fluid
    - Ideal for cinematic footage or preparing clips for broadcast requirements
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Best Practices

<Info>
**Pro Tips for Best Results:**

- **For images**: Upscale step by step (4K → 8K → 22K) to preserve fidelity
- **For videos**: Use Frame Interpolation at 60 fps for smooth results; use 90–120 fps for slow motion
- **Enable Fix Duplicate Frames** if you notice flickering or repeated frames
- **Use Grain** for a more natural cinematic look in upscaled footage
</Info>
